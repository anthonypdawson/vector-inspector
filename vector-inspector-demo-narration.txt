🎙️ A clean, confident narration outline
1. Opening (5–10 seconds)
Something simple like:
“I started building Vector Inspector with Chroma as the first provider, because it gave me a fast way to prototype and validate the core workflows.”

This sets the tone without overselling.

2. Connection flow (10–15 seconds)
As you create the connection:
“Here I’m connecting to a local Chroma database. File‑based workflows were the first thing I wanted to support, because they’re the most frictionless way to experiment.”

This subtly highlights a pain point without criticizing them.

3. Browsing a collection (10–15 seconds)
As you click into a collection:
“Once connected, you can browse collections, inspect metadata, and get a feel for the structure of your data.”

This shows value without explaining every pixel.

4. Data view (10–15 seconds)
Scrolling the table:
“The data view gives you IDs, metadata, and a preview of the embeddings so you can quickly understand what’s inside your vector store.”

This is the “developer tool” moment.

5. Search (10–15 seconds)
Running a semantic query:
“You can run semantic searches directly against the collection, which makes it easy to validate embeddings or debug retrieval behavior.”

This is where they realize you’re solving real developer pain.

6. Visualization (10–15 seconds)
Switching to the scatterplot:
“And there’s a built‑in visualization that lets you explore the embedding space interactively.”

This is the “wow” moment.

7. Closing (5 seconds)
Something light:
“Still early, but this should give you a sense of where I’m taking it.”

Short, humble, confident.
